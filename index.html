    Malta experienced the fastest five-year average population growth in the EU27, with an increase of 3.2% from 1st January 2020.


  • Expats Share in Working Population
    • Expats Share in Working Population
    ×

    In 2020, approximately 25% of the total employed population in Malta consisted of expatriates. Among these expats, 51.2% were EU nationals, while 48.8% were third country nationals. The main countries of origin for expats in Malta were Italy, accounting for 15.4% of the expat population, followed by the United Kingdom at 8.1%. Other significant contributors were the Philippines at 7.8%, Serbia at 6.9%, India at 6.4%, and Bulgaria at 3.9%.

    EU 27 Country and Commonwealth

    Malta became a member of the European Union in 2004 and adopted the euro as its currency in 2008, becoming a member of the Eurozone. Additionally, Malta is a member of the Commonwealth and actively participates in this international organization.




    Currency

    Since 2008, Malta has been utilizing the Euro as its official currency.


    Languages

    Maltese and English are the official languages in Malta, with English primarily used in business settings. A large portion of the Maltese population is bilingual, and many individuals also speak an additional language.

    Days of Sun and Entertainment

    Malta experiences over 300 sunny days per year, with mild winters and hot, dry summers. This climate provides ample opportunities for people to enjoy a range of outdoor activities, from relaxing on the beach to taking walks in the countryside. Malta is also known for its vibrant entertainment areas, offering renowned venues where the nightlife is lively throughout the week.


    History, Arts and Culture

    The Maltese islands present a rich tapestry of history that reflects the diverse influences of the civilizations that have occupied Malta over time. Malta boasts three UNESCO World Heritage sites, including the world's oldest free-standing temples, dating back to 3600 B.C. This historical legacy showcases the fascinating cultural heritage and ancient wonders found on the islands of Malta.

  • Safe Destination
    • A Safe Destination
    ×

    Malta is recognized as one of the safest destinations globally, offering a sense of security where individuals can freely explore without personal safety concerns. The country has also managed the COVID-19 pandemic effectively, boasting one of the highest rates of testing and vaccination worldwide. Unlike many other places, Malta did not enforce a complete lockdown during the pandemic, showcasing its proactive approach in handling the situation while prioritizing public health.

    The Maltese economy is characterized by a strong diversification, which plays a crucial role in ensuring resilience against adverse shocks. This diversification, coupled with sound economic policies, has enabled Malta to recover quickly after recessions and achieve a faster growth rate compared to its European Union counterparts. It is worth noting that the total Gross Value Added (GVA) in 2019 amounted to €12 billion.
